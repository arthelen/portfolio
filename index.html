Double Exposure Edit

This self-directed piece explores double exposure—a technique where two images are blended to form a visually compelling composite. I combined a profile photo of Felix (Stray Kids) with a vibrant bouquet of flowers to symbolize natural beauty and growth. The composition balances softness and strength while experimenting with masking and layering effects for a surreal finish. This piece was created as part of a digital design class focused on conceptual creativity and technical execution.

Wireframe

This is a high-fidelity wireframe I created for a class project centered on user-centered design. The concept was a fictitious website called Glutino, created to help users easily explore gluten-free recipes. The design focuses on clean organization and user accessibility, with a layout that displays recipes in rows of four and allows users to filter by meal type, preparation time, and difficulty level. A flower-based difficulty rating system visually indicates how easy or challenging each recipe is, making it simple for users to choose based on their preferences. Soft pinks and yellows help create a warm, welcoming feel that aligns with the theme of healthy eating and dietary mindfulness. I included detailed annotations to explain the functionality and layout choices, showcasing both design thinking and practical UX decisions throughout the process.
